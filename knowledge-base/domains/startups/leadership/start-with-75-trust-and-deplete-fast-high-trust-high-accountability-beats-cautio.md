Tobi Lutke (Shopify CEO) famously starts new hires at a 50% trust battery that builds over time. Nejatian inverts this: he starts at 75% trust but depletes it much faster. The reasoning is that starting with low trust means you learn too slowly about what people are capable of. If you assume a new teammate is bad, you'll overcompensate and never see their real capacity.

The hockey team analogy makes it concrete: if you're the new left winger, assuming the centerman is bad means you'll play out of position and never learn his strengths. Better to assume competence, allocate risk early ('I'm underwriting you to take the risk'), and then observe results with honest eyes. The result at Opendoor: only one member of the pre-Kaz executive team remains two quarters later. High trust up front, but fast accountability — strong attract, strong repel.
