One of the most common traps for novice leaders is believing they need to know more technically than every team member. This drives two destructive behaviors: first, they spend excessive time studying to maintain technical superiority rather than leading; second, they unconsciously avoid hiring anyone smarter than themselves, which systematically lowers the team's talent density.

The result is self-reinforcing: with weaker team members, everything flows to the leader, confirming the false belief that they need to know everything. A leader's actual role is to clearly present a direction and select people who can lead in that direction — not to be the answer to every question. If everyone on your team comes to you for answers, that's not proof of your competence; it's proof your team's talent density is too low.
