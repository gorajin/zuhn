Evans articulates a fundamental pattern of platform shifts: the Apple II couldn't run banks, Netscape couldn't do professional video editing, mobile couldn't replace a 5-screen programming rig — but 5 billion people got smartphones while only 800 million had consumer PCs. Each new platform was terrible at what the old thing excelled at, but unlocked entirely new behaviors.

Applied to AI: obsessing over whether generative AI can replace existing workflows misses the point. The real question is what new things become possible that you would never have done before. Online dating, ride-sharing, and short-form video were new behaviors enabled by mobile, not better versions of existing ones. The AI equivalent of these new behaviors hasn't fully emerged yet, and that's where the biggest companies will be built.
