Animals in the wild rarely get chronic diseases. Put them in a zoo and they become obese, depressed, develop strange behaviors, and in some cases even kill themselves. The insight: an organism's health is a reflection of its environment's health, not the organism's willpower.

Applied to humans: the modern American environment — ultra-processed food, 8+ hours on phones, children spending less time outside than maximum-security prisoners — is a species-inappropriate environment. Trying to be healthy through individual discipline in this environment is like asking a zoo animal to just 'be more like its wild counterpart.' The leverage point is reshaping the environment itself, which is why community-level health interventions (health-oriented neighborhoods, group health insurance for people living differently) may be far more effective than individual optimization.
