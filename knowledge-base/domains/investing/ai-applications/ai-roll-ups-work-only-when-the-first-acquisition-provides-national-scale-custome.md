The naive AI roll-up thesis — buy 200 accounting firms and inject AI — fails because each local acquisition only gives local clients and requires 9 months of integration before buying the next. This is traditional PE territory where mid-market firms have done it 500 times.

The interesting version is buying a single stagnant company with blue-chip clients at 3x EBITDA, then transforming it with AI and scaling nationally from that customer base. A declining debt collection company with five blue-chip clients becomes your sales channel — you transform their operations with AI, demonstrate better collection rates, and onboard a thousand new clients without buying a second company. The key test: is the business inherently national (debt collection, IT services) or inherently local (dental clinics, dermatology offices)? Only national businesses support the buy-one-transform-many strategy.
