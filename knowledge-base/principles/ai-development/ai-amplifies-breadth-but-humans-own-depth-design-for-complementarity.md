AI excels at breadth (trying every technique on every problem, generating ideas at scale, handling auxiliary tasks) while humans excel at depth (cumulative reasoning, deleting assumptions, building from partial progress). The highest-value systems don't replace one with the other — they redesign workflows so AI maps the landscape and humans dig into the hard spots. Tao's math papers are richer but not deeper. Karpathy's agents move mountains but can't build cumulative handholds. MIT found the best use cases unlock capabilities, not just speed.
