Rather than waiting for insurance companies to cover lifestyle interventions or lobbying for new legislation, Truemed identified an existing IRS mechanism — letters of medical necessity — that already allowed tax-free HSA/FSA spending on qualifying health interventions. They built telemedicine infrastructure to issue these letters at scale.

This is a powerful startup pattern: instead of trying to change the system, find existing rails that already permit what you want to do, then build infrastructure to make them accessible. Truemed partnered with brands like Eight Sleep, Peloton, and Lifetime Fitness, enabling customers at risk of chronic disease to use pre-tax dollars on lifestyle interventions. The potential is hundreds of billions redirected toward prevention using mechanisms that already exist.
