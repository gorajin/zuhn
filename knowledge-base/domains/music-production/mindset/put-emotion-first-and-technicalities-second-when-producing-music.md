Producers are storytellers, not sound designers. Nobody in a nightclub or on a long drive appreciates your multiband mid-range compression. They connect to emotional arc, melody, rhythm, and groove. Spending years mastering sound design while ignoring emotional impact is building the wrong skill.

The mindset shift: think like a listener, not a producer. Ask 'does this transition feel right?' not 'is this technically perfect?' Study songs you listen to repeatedly -- what brings you back is tension and release, energy dynamics, and emotional connection, not technical wizardry. Technical skill serves emotion, never the reverse.
