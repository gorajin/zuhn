Andreessen identifies a structural economic force: the price of AI per unit of intelligence is collapsing faster than Moore's law ever drove chip costs down. This is happening across the entire cost structure — chips, energy, model efficiency, serving costs — simultaneously.

The economic implication follows classic demand elasticity: as tokens-by-the-drink get dramatically cheaper, demand will grow more than proportionally. GPU shortages will become GPU gluts (the number one cause of a shortage is a glut, and vice versa). AI chips from AMD, hyperscaler custom silicon, and Chinese manufacturers will proliferate within 5 years. For builders and investors, this means the economics of AI applications will improve radically — companies that look unprofitable today at current compute costs may become highly profitable as the cost floor drops. The strategic play is to build for value capture now while costs are high, knowing margins will expand as infrastructure costs deflate.
