Alex Rampell (a16z) presents a framework for the AI application investment landscape, identifying three key categories: traditional software going AI-native (greenfield vs brownfield), software eating labor (replacing human jobs with AI at lower cost-to-value ratios), and walled gardens (proprietary data assets made dramatically more valuable by adding AI). Uses case studies of Eve (legal AI), Salient (auto loan servicing), and data companies like FlightAware and VLex to illustrate moat-building strategies.
